Introduction

Metal 3D printing revolutionizes manufacturing by enabling the creation of complex, high-performance metal parts with precision and efficiency, unlocking new possibilities in aerospace, automotive, medical, and other industries.

DMLS Printing

Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS) is an additive manufacturing process that uses a high-powered laser to selectively fuse metal powder particles into a solid object. It enables the creation of complex and fully functional metal parts with high precision.

Applications :

  • Aerospace and Defense: DMLS is widely used in the aerospace and defense industries for manufacturing lightweight and intricate components, such as turbine blades, brackets, and engine parts. It allows for the production of parts with high strength and excellent heat resistance.
  • Automotive: DMLS is utilized in the automotive industry for producing complex and customized parts, including engine components, brackets, and lightweight structural parts. It enables rapid prototyping and on-demand manufacturing.
  • Medical and Dental: DMLS is applied in the medical and dental fields for manufacturing patient-specific implants, surgical instruments, orthopedic implants, and dental prosthetics. It offers high precision and biocompatible materials suitable for medical applications.
  • Tooling and Manufacturing: DMLS is used for producing high-performance tooling inserts, molds, and fixtures. It enables the production of complex geometries and customized tooling with reduced lead times.
  • Jewelry and Art: DMLS technology is also employed in the jewelry and art sectors for creating intricate and detailed metal pieces. It allows for the production of unique and complex designs that are difficult to achieve through traditional manufacturing methods.

SLS Printing

Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) is an additive manufacturing process that utilizes a high-powered laser to selectively fuse powdered materials, typically polymers or metals, into a solid object. The laser selectively fuses the powdered particles together layer by layer, creating a three-dimensional object.

Applications :

  • Functional Prototypes: SLS printing is widely used for producing functional prototypes, especially when complex geometries and high mechanical strength are required. It allows for the creation of parts with good accuracy and a wide range of material options.
  • Manufacturing End-Use Parts: SLS is utilized in small-scale manufacturing for producing end-use parts, particularly in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and consumer goods. It enables the production of parts with excellent strength and durability.
  • Tooling and Jigs: SLS is suitable for producing tooling components like molds, jigs, and fixtures. The ability to manufacture these parts quickly and cost-effectively makes SLS beneficial for tooling applications.
  • Customized Medical Devices: SLS printing is applied in the medical field for manufacturing customized prosthetics, orthotics, surgical guides, and anatomical models. It allows for patient-specific designs and rapid production.
  • Art and Design: SLS technology is also used in art and design fields to create intricate sculptures, artistic pieces, and architectural models.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$100 to $500/liter

EBM Printing

Electron Beam Melting (EBM) is an additive manufacturing process that utilizes an electron beam to selectively melt and fuse metal powders together, layer by layer, to create fully dense metal objects. EBM technology operates in a vacuum environment, allowing for precise control over the melting and solidification of the metal powders.

Applications :

  • Aerospace and Defense: EBM is commonly used in the aerospace and defense industries for manufacturing complex and high-performance components, such as turbine blades, aerospace engine parts, and structural elements. The process enables the production of parts with superior mechanical properties and reduced material waste.
  • Medical and Dental: EBM finds applications in the medical and dental fields for producing patient-specific implants, orthopedic devices, and dental prosthetics. The ability to create customized, biocompatible metal parts with complex geometries makes EBM valuable in this industry.
  • Tooling and Mold Making: EBM is employed in tooling and mold-making applications for creating durable and high-quality tool inserts, molds, and dies. The process allows for the production of complex tooling components with improved heat resistance and wear properties.
  • Research and Development: EBM technology is widely used in research and development settings to explore new materials, study metal properties, and develop advanced applications. Researchers can experiment with different alloys and optimize material properties using EBM.
  • Energy and Power Generation: EBM is utilized in the energy and power generation sectors for manufacturing components used in gas turbines, nuclear reactors, and other energy-related equipment. The process allows for the production of intricate and high-temperature-resistant parts.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$500 to $2000/kg

Binder Jetting Printing

Binder Jetting is an additive manufacturing process that involves depositing layers of powdered material, such as metal, ceramic, or sand, and selectively binding the particles together using a liquid binder. The process creates green parts that require further post-processing, such as curing or sintering, to achieve their final strength and properties.

Applications :

  • Sand Casting and Foundry: Binder Jetting is commonly used in the foundry industry for producing sand molds and cores. It allows for the creation of complex and detailed molds with reduced lead times and cost compared to traditional casting methods.
  • Tooling and Jigs: Binder Jetting is employed for creating tooling components, jigs, and fixtures used in manufacturing. It enables the production of customized tooling with intricate geometries and reduced turnaround times.
  • Architectural Models: Binder Jetting technology finds applications in the architectural and construction fields for creating detailed scale models of buildings, landscapes, and urban planning projects. It allows architects and designers to visualize their designs in a cost-effective and time-efficient manner.
  • Medical and Dental: Binder Jetting is utilized in the medical and dental sectors for producing patient-specific anatomical models, dental molds, and prosthetics. It enables the production of customized and intricate designs required for surgical planning and patient-specific treatments.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Binder Jetting is suitable for rapid prototyping applications across various industries. It allows for the quick and cost-effective production of prototypes with relatively high accuracy and surface finish.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$50 to $200/kg
