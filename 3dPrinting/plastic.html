Introduction

3D printing with plastics revolutionizes manufacturing by enabling the creation of three-dimensional objects through additive processes, offering endless possibilities for prototyping, customization, and small-scale production.

FDM Printing

3D FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) printing is an additive manufacturing process that uses a filament material, typically thermoplastics, to create three-dimensional objects layer by layer.

Applications :

  • Prototyping: FDM printing is widely used in prototyping due to its ability to quickly produce functional prototypes, allowing for design verification and iteration.
  • Manufacturing: FDM printing is increasingly being used in small-scale manufacturing to produce end-use parts, especially for low-volume production runs or customized products.
  • Education: FDM printers are commonly used in educational settings to teach students about design, engineering, and manufacturing concepts, providing hands-on experience.
  • Architecture and Construction: FDM printing is utilized to create architectural models, construction components, and even entire buildings using large-scale printers.
  • Medical Field: FDM printing is used in the medical field for creating customized prosthetics, surgical tools, anatomical models, and patient-specific implants.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$0.10/foot

SLA Printing

Stereolithography (SLA) is an additive manufacturing process that uses a liquid photopolymer resin and a UV laser to create three-dimensional objects layer by layer. The resin is selectively cured by the laser, solidifying it and building up the object.

Applications :

  • Prototyping: SLA printing is commonly used in prototyping for high-resolution models, intricate designs, and smooth surface finishes. It allows for precise detailing and can replicate complex geometries accurately.
  • Jewelry and Art: SLA printers are popular in the jewelry industry and art communities due to their ability to produce intricate and detailed pieces with a high level of precision.
  • Dental and Orthodontics: SLA printing is widely used in the dental field for fabricating dental models, surgical guides, clear aligners, crowns, and bridges. Its accuracy and ability to produce custom-fit dental appliances make it valuable in this industry.
  • Engineering and Manufacturing: SLA printing finds applications in engineering and manufacturing for creating functional prototypes, molds, and tooling. It enables rapid iteration and verification of designs before transitioning to traditional manufacturing processes.
  • Aerospace and Automotive: SLA printing is utilized in aerospace and automotive industries for creating lightweight components, intricate parts, and prototypes for wind tunnel testing and functional validation.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$50 - $150/volume

SLS Printing

Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) is an additive manufacturing process that utilizes a high-powered laser to selectively fuse powdered materials, typically polymers or metals, into a solid object. The laser selectively fuses the powdered particles together layer by layer, creating a three-dimensional object.

Applications :

  • Functional Prototypes: SLS printing is widely used for producing functional prototypes, especially when complex geometries and high mechanical strength are required. It allows for the creation of parts with good accuracy and a wide range of material options.
  • Manufacturing End-Use Parts: SLS is utilized in small-scale manufacturing for producing end-use parts, particularly in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and consumer goods. It enables the production of parts with excellent strength and durability.
  • Tooling and Jigs: SLS is suitable for producing tooling components like molds, jigs, and fixtures. The ability to manufacture these parts quickly and cost-effectively makes SLS beneficial for tooling applications.
  • Customized Medical Devices: SLS printing is applied in the medical field for manufacturing customized prosthetics, orthotics, surgical guides, and anatomical models. It allows for patient-specific designs and rapid production.
  • Art and Design: SLS technology is also used in art and design fields to create intricate sculptures, artistic pieces, and architectural models.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$100 to $500/liter

PolyJet Printing

PolyJet is an additive manufacturing technology that utilizes inkjet printing principles to create three-dimensional objects. It involves jetting photopolymer materials in liquid form onto a build tray and then curing them with UV light, layer by layer, to produce highly detailed and smooth models.

Applications :

  • Product Design and Prototyping: PolyJet is widely used in product design and prototyping to create highly accurate and realistic models with complex geometries, intricate details, and smooth surface finishes. It enables designers to validate designs and make iterations before proceeding to production.
  • Medical and Dental Applications: PolyJet printing finds extensive applications in the medical and dental fields. It is used to create anatomical models, surgical guides, dental models, orthodontic aligners, and prosthetics. The high level of detail and accuracy of PolyJet makes it valuable in creating patient-specific medical devices.
  • Architecture and Visualizations: PolyJet technology is employed in architecture for creating detailed models of buildings, landscapes, and urban planning. It allows architects to visualize their designs accurately and communicate ideas effectively.
  • Education and Research: PolyJet printers are used in educational institutions and research facilities to teach and explore various disciplines, such as engineering, product design, biology, and materials science. It enables students and researchers to work with tangible models and prototypes.

Print Time:

Anywhere from a few minutes to a few days depending of the size of the project.

Material price:

$10 - $50/liter
